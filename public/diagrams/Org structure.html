Spikad Organisation Structure

Clear command structure from top to bottom. One Architect, one CEO, and one owner per production stage. This is how we avoid agency chaos and operate as a company factory.

Omar — Founder & Chief Architect

Owns the system, the product, and the exit.

  • Designs and evolves the production line system
  • Defines packages, guarantees, and exclusions
  • Builds and owns the Spikad platform
  • Designs the exit strategy (Fortnox / Visma / VC)
  • Does not run daily operations or client delivery
Designs the machine

Ayham — CEO / Factory Manager

Runs the factory and enforces discipline.

  • Owns company-wide KPIs and production performance
  • Manages flow between Intake → Formation → Build → Handover
  • Identifies and removes bottlenecks
  • Allocates resources between stages
  • Enforces no-exceptions and scope discipline
Runs the machine

Nidal — Head of Intake & Growth

Stage Owner: Intake

  • Manages sales pipeline and first client calls
  • Runs SOME & marketing campaigns (with interns)
  • Generates and qualifies leads
  • Matches clients to the correct package
  • Creates and locks the Build Map
  • Does not deliver websites or design

Malek — Head of Formation & Accounting

Stage Owner: Formation

  • Registers EF / AB companies
  • Handles F-skatt, VAT, and compliance
  • Sets up accounting systems
  • Runs ongoing monthly accounting
  • Manages junior accountants
  • Rejects incomplete or risky setups

Ammar — Head of Build & Quality

Stage Owner: Build

  • Owns quality of branding, websites, and SOME assets
  • Decides final design direction
  • Manages Elias and external developers
  • Ensures delivery matches the Build Map
  • Prevents rework and scope drift

Nana — Head of Delivery & Accounts

Stage Owner: Handover

  • Main client communication during delivery
  • Handles feedback and routes Change Orders
  • Delivers final assets and access
  • Manages client accounts post-delivery
  • Ensures clean project closure
Non-negotiable rule: One owner per stage. No cross-stage work. No exceptions. Everything else becomes a new order.
